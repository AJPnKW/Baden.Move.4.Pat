# Coordination Thread

## Project

- Name: Baden move and transition project
- Delivery style: lightweight, hybrid exploratory plus cutover coordination

## Project Framing

- This was not a simple relocation task
- The project combined sale preparation, staged move execution, renovation planning, occupancy-readiness thinking, and service cutover coordination
- The management approach stayed intentionally lightweight but became more structured where dependencies tightened

## Workstream View

### Sale-Side Readiness

- staging for photos
- staging for viewings
- managing what stayed visible, what moved out, and what was deferred

### New-Home Readiness

- renovation option analysis
- bathroom and laundry related decision pressure
- furnishing and fixture selection work

### Move Execution

- mover planning
- self-move planning
- overlap occupancy sequencing
- day-specific execution control

### Support And Continuity

- utility and connectivity coordination
- essential-room stabilization
- continuity planning for household support needs

## Sample Open-Issue Pattern

| Issue Type | What It Looked Like | Response Style |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Emerging scope | New information changed what counted as in-scope | Reframe and update boundaries |
| Deadline pressure | A choice had to be made before all information was ideal | Make bounded decision and document rationale |
| Dependency conflict | One workstream blocked or reshaped another | Escalate to phase-level planning |
| Cutover risk | Partial execution could break the overall sequence | Use tighter task and date control |

## Sample Next-Actions Pattern

- identify the next decision that could not be deferred
- separate exploratory tasks from execution-locked tasks
- document what had to be true before the next phase could begin
- keep the working record concise and usable
