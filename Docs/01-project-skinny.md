# Project Skinny

## Problem

Coordinate a home transition project that was larger than a simple move. The work combined sale preparation, move planning, renovation readiness, staged occupancy overlap, furnishing and fixture selection, household service cutovers, and decision-heavy transition work under real dates and changing information.

## Output

A lightweight but structured move-and-transition plan with phased activities, decision points, issue handling, cutover-style checkpoints, and clear boundaries between exploratory work and must-land execution work.

## Success Definition

The old home is prepared for sale activity, the new home is made ready in the right sequence, critical dates are protected, dependencies are surfaced early enough to act on them, and the move is stabilized without key tasks being missed.

## Why PM-Light Was Appropriate

This project did not need a large formal project-management framework, but it did need more than ad hoc note-taking.

The work involved:

- multiple parallel workstreams
- fixed date pressure
- interdependency between renovation and occupancy
- frequent scope clarification
- decisions that had to be made before full information was available

That made a PM-light approach the right fit: small enough to live with, structured enough to control risk.
